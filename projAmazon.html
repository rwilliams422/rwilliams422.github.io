Amazon Seller Website Redesign

Date Fall 2021

Project Tools: Microsoft Word, TechSmith Camtasia, Figma

Research Methods: Task Analysis and Think Aloud Protcol

Abstract: Amazon Sellers website consists of poor layout and confusing information for its users. Through the Usability Research methods of task analysis and think aloud protocol I was able to determine what what needed to be the most accessible for the user and how the layout needs to align with Norman's Principles of design. Proposed changes for the cite are created by app prototyping in Figma to meet the needs of the user. The proposed changes meet the principles of design, visibility, feedback, affordance, mapping, constraints, and consistency.

Project Proposal

Object of study

Website: https://sellercentral.amazon.com/

Note: a login is needed to make and see the page outside of the login page. A Screenshot of object of study are located at the end of the of the memo.

Summary of website

This website is for amazon sellers and it documents all the information that is needed for an amazon seller. It has the listing of items they are trying to sell. There are news articles for sellers, helpful tips and even a “Seller University” with links and tips on how to sell. There is a navbar across the top with Catalog, inventory, pricing, orders, advertising, stores, growth, reports, performance, partner Network and 2B2. The Navbar is partly hidden by the boxes at the top including Total Balance, Today’s Sales, Marketplaces etc.

Audience

The audience of this website is amazon sellers. This is not a cite you can see unless you make and get approved for an Amazon sellers account. The other audience and users of this site would be the developers and mainly the IT people dealing with Amazon sellers who call in with confusing questions. (There are a lot of these because of the poor communication of information on the cite).

Editing

I have been using the Amazon Sellers account for the past several months and the UI is not only poorly looking but it is poorly designed. Important features like the items you have listed are not easily accessible. The overall UI is cumbersome and looks like it is simply a rough draft. The visual cues on the page are confusing, and in some ways not even there on the page. The header is very small and doesn’t give the visual cue of “the top”. These are just some of the issues that need to be addressed in this website. Editing this would allow me to conduct Usability Research on what the current state of the site and reaching out for comments from friends of mine who sell on Amazon sellers. The end project would include app prototyping that includes a better overall UI design which incorporates the UX research that was conducted. This mockup wouldn’t have database information from the website, but it would have stand in information. Before making that mock-up, I would also have simple pdf documents that would design the UI prototype. Additionally, for text editing, there would be changes made to the “News” Section. The information they are trying to convey is important, but it is wordy and not explained well. Making these changes would create better communication for those using the cite.

UX Research

I took two participants one who has never used the website before and one who has, to get different feedback in terms of completing tasks that amazon sellers need to be able to navigate quickly throughout the process.

Some tasks I asked them to completes included,

Can you check your messages?

See all the past orders that have been shipped in thelast 365 days,

Add an item to your sellers account

For the second research method, think aloud protocol I asked them questions in terms of Norman's Principles of design

Questions What do you see on the Home Screen in terms of Visibility? What do you see on the Home Screen in terms of feedback? What do you see on the Home Screen in terms of Affordance? What do you see on the Home Screen in terms of Mapping? Ask: What do you see on the Home Screen in terms of Constraints? Ask: What do you see on the Home Screen in terms of Consistency?

Conclusion

In this project I learned a lot about the design process of editing. Content editing for visuals was more overwhelming than I expected it to be. Here is on concluding analysis of the new prototype in terms of Norman's Design principles.

Visibility

This new UI design creates better visibility for the user. While everything was there before like the participants of my study said, now the information is more direct, and the extraneous and distracting things are no longer there causing blockage. Everything is seen on the screen that we need as a user toc complete and get to a task.

Feedback

The website had extremely good feedback when it came to completion of tasks. I found that I did not need to make any changes in this area.

Affordance

The changes I made on this site create a much better affordance. I was able to see how something is used based on what it looks like. For example, the help center was just a button before. But now based on what you see you have a visual cue that in the help center you will type you questions and speak with an agent. The same can be said for other elements on the home screen.

Mapping

Mapping on this cite is much better now. We can see that on the home screen we don’t have a scroll bar because there isn’t a bunch of wasted information at the bottom. The framework of the space overall is there and is consistent through.

Constraints

During the study, a test user said, “They have a lot of options that feel like they all do the same thing.” This was something I struggled with a lot as well in the process of using this site. With the new layout we have fewer confusing constraints. There are less limits for the interactions a user will have with the cite.

Consistency

The site had poor consistence before the changes. Now we have brighter colors, better font consistence’s and things like the navbar running across the entire top of the screen. We see the consistency continue we go to the more news section and the same navbar across the top and the bottom bar are the same as they are on the home screen.
