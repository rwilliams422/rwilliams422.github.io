Problem Solving: Students Next Steps

Date Winter 2022

Project Tools: Canva and Indesign

Research Methods: Directed Storytelling, surveys, focus groups, and A/B testing

Abstract: Students in the Professional and Technical Writing program at Saginaw Valley State University were entering further into the program lacking information they needed to succeed. The goal of this project was to create a realistic solution that solved the problem which could be implemented into the program. The target audience was students who had already started the program but needed pieces of information. After completing a focus group and survey to students in the program we focused on five areas that students need more information including, the labs on campus and what internship is like. We created five documents that addressed the issues. These documents are now used by professors in the department and the department head for students.

Problem Statement

Within our department there is a lack of communication to the students about resources and department information that ultimately leads to frustration from students and lack of involvement.

Personas

At the beginning of the project, we made Personas to outline the responses we got from our survey. We split the results into two “people” so we could visualize the responses based on students who were at the end of the program and students who were new to the program. We took this feedback and used it to shape the information we put on the documents.

Who are the stakeholders?

Midway through the project we looked at who our documents would be used by. At the top we have our primary audience, students new to the program but other people will use the documents. The stakeholder map makes sure you verify who your entire audience is for your project. Making sure terminology makes sense for all these user groups and that the primary (top row) and secondary (second top) will understand the information in the documents and why its important.
